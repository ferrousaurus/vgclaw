## Team Analysis

### Identity

This is a dual-mode balance team built around `Aegislash + Ninetales-Alola`. The primary mode is veil-supported mid-speed pressure with `Garchomp` and `Incineroar`. The fallback mode is a compact Trick Room shell with `Farigiraf + Sinistcha`, letting `Aegislash` operate as a cleaner instead of forcing it into every fast board state.

### Main Modes

- `Ninetales-Alola / Garchomp / Aegislash / Incineroar`
  Veil balance. Lead for early tempo with `Aurora Veil`, `Fake Out`, and immediate pressure from `Garchomp` or `Aegislash`.
- `Farigiraf / Sinistcha / Aegislash / Incineroar`
  Trick Room fallback. Use this when opposing fast offense or priority-heavy teams make the snow mode too fragile.
- `Ninetales-Alola / Garchomp / Incineroar / Farigiraf`
  Hybrid anti-offense. Bring this when you want veil plus the threat of a late Trick Room pivot.

### Roles Covered

- Speed control: `Icy Wind`, `Trick Room`
- Intimidate / Attack drops: `Incineroar`, `Strength Sap`
- Redirection: `Sinistcha`
- Fake Out: `Incineroar`
- Setup denial: `Taunt`
- Spread damage: `Blizzard`, `Rock Slide`, `Hyper Voice`
- Weather: `Snow Warning`

The team does not use Tailwind. That is acceptable here because the structure is not trying to race the entire format in one mode. It instead uses `Aurora Veil`, Fake Out cycling, and Trick Room inversion to control pace.

### Win Conditions

- `Aurora Veil` enabling `Garchomp` and `Aegislash` to trade favorably into midgame
- `Aegislash` cleaning with `Poltergeist + Shadow Sneak` once faster threats are chipped
- `Farigiraf + Sinistcha` establishing Trick Room so `Aegislash` and `Incineroar` can win low-speed boards
- `Incineroar` creating endgames through `Fake Out`, `Parting Shot`, and Intimidate loops

The team has two real paths to winning. It is not all-in on snow, and it is not all-in on Trick Room.

### Matchup Notes

#### Into fast offense

- Default to `Ninetales-Alola + Incineroar` or `Ninetales-Alola + Garchomp` leads.
- `Aurora Veil` plus `Yache Berry` gives `Garchomp` room to survive opposing Ice pressure once.
- If the opponent is too aggressive for veil to stabilize, pivot to the `Farigiraf + Sinistcha` mode.

#### Into Incineroar-heavy balance

- `Aegislash` is the key threat because Ghost pressure ignores Intimidate.
- `Sacred Sword` gives `Aegislash` direct counterplay into Dark- and Steel-leaning boards.
- Avoid overcommitting `Garchomp` early if they are clearly preserving Ice coverage for it.

#### Into priority-heavy teams

- `Armor Tail` on `Farigiraf` is the reason the Trick Room mode exists.
- This is the cleanest route when `Shadow Sneak`, `Sucker Punch`, or other priority would otherwise cut off your endgame.

#### Into spread-heavy teams

- `Aegislash` can be preserved for the late game instead of forced early.
- `Aurora Veil` matters more than raw damage in these games.
- `Sinistcha` gives you a slower sustain line if trading fast is unfavorable.

### Main Risks

- The team is still somewhat Ice-sensitive if `Ninetales-Alola` is lost before veil goes up and `Garchomp` is brought carelessly.
- It lacks a second Fake Out user or secondary Intimidate slot, so `Incineroar` is a high-value support piece.
- The fast mode depends on good tempo management rather than raw speed. If veil is denied and Trick Room is also prevented, the team can be forced into awkward mid-speed trades.
