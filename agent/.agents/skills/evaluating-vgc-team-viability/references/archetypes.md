# VGC Team Archetypes

> **Champions Rules Reminder**
> - All IVs are fixed to 31. Never reference 0 IVs or IV manipulation.
> - Stat Points (SPs) replace EVs: 1 SP = 8 EVs, max 32 per stat, 66 total.
> - Damage is deterministic — no random rolls.
> - Level 50 for all VGC calculations.
> - Item pool is limited: no Choice Band, Choice Specs, Life Orb, Assault Vest, Safety Goggles, etc.

> **When to read this file:** This reference is invoked primarily by the **Archetype Coherence** dimension in `SKILL.md`. Commonly read alongside `tempo.md` (archetypes imply lead patterns) and `items.md` (weather teams may need specific items; Mega Evolution can patch archetype weaknesses).

How to use this file: identify the team's archetype by checking for the defining elements below. Then score **Archetype Coherence** using the anchors at the bottom of this file.

## Rain

**Core:** Rain setter (Drizzle ability or Rain Dance move) + Swift Swim attacker or rain-boosted moves user.

**Strategy:** Rain setter activates rain. Swift Swim users double Speed. Water moves get 50% boost, Fire moves weakened. Hurricane/Thunder become 100% accurate.

**Key Moves:** Muddy Water, Hydro Pump, Hurricane, Thunder, Weather Ball

**Strengths:** Fast and powerful Water offense, weakens Fire-types, reliable accuracy moves
**Weaknesses:** Opposing weather (Sand, Sun), Grass-types, Water Absorb/Storm Drain, Electric-types

**Champions profile:** Needs a rain setter, at least one Swift Swim or rain abuser, and ideally a secondary mode that doesn't depend on rain.

## Sun

**Core:** Sun setter (Drought ability or Sunny Day move) + Chlorophyll user or sun abusers.

**Strategy:** Sun setter activates sun. Chlorophyll users double Speed. Fire moves boosted 50%, Water weakened. Solar Beam has no charge turn. Growth gives a double boost in sun.

**Key Moves:** Eruption, Heat Wave, Solar Beam, Growth, Weather Ball

**Strengths:** Massive Fire damage via Eruption, weakens Water, Solar Beam covers Water/Ground/Rock
**Weaknesses:** Rock-type attacks, opposing weather, Rain teams, Flash Fire

**Champions profile:** Needs a sun setter, at least one Chlorophyll or Fire abuser, and a non-sun backup attacker.

## Sand

**Core:** Sand setter (Sand Stream ability or Sandstorm move) + Sand Rush user or Rock/Ground/Steel abusers.

**Strategy:** Sand Stream sets sandstorm passively. Sand Rush users double Speed. Rock/Ground/Steel types get Sp.Def boost in sand.

**Key Moves:** Rock Slide, Earthquake, Iron Head, High Horsepower, Protect

**Strengths:** Strong physical offense, Sp.Def boost for Rock-types, Sand Rush speed
**Weaknesses:** Water, Grass, Fighting, opposing weather, Wide Guard

**Champions profile:** Needs a sand setter, at least one Sand Rush or sand abuser, and coverage for Water/Grass weaknesses.

## Snow / Hail

**Core:** Snow setter (Snow Warning ability) + Aurora Veil user or Slush Rush abusers.

**Strategy:** Snow Warning sets snow. Aurora Veil reduces damage for 5 turns. Blizzard becomes 100% accurate.

**Key Moves:** Blizzard, Aurora Veil, Ice Shard

**Strengths:** Aurora Veil is very strong defensively, Blizzard is powerful spread move
**Weaknesses:** Weakest weather offensively, many Ice-type weaknesses, opposing weather

**Champions profile:** Needs a snow setter, at least one abuser, and a plan for the numerous Fire/Fighting/Rock/Steel weaknesses.

## Trick Room

**Core:** Trick Room setter + slow heavy hitters.

**Strategy:** Reverse Speed for 5 turns. Slowest Pokemon move first. Build around Pokemon with very low Speed and high offenses.

**Key Moves:** Trick Room, Close Combat, Gyro Ball, Eruption under TR with Torkoal

**Strengths:** Flips speed advantage, slow Pokemon hit extremely hard, fast opponents become liabilities
**Weaknesses:** Limited to 5 turns, Taunt blocks setup, opposing Trick Room, Imprison

**Champions profile:** Needs a bulky TR setter (preferably with Mental Herb or Magic Bounce), 2-3 slow attackers (base Speed ≤ 45), and a fast backup mode for when TR is denied.

## Hyper Offense

**Core:** Fast, hard-hitting Pokemon with setup or immediate pressure.

**Strategy:** Overwhelm the opponent with damage before they can respond. Less reliance on defensive pivoting.

**Key Moves:** Fake Out, Tailwind, Swords Dance, Protect, powerful STAB attacks

**Strengths:** Forces opponent to react, punishes passive play, less concerned with longevity
**Weaknesses:** Intimidate, priority moves, opposing speed control, needs to win quickly

**Champions profile:** Expects multiple independent threats. Pair Synergy and Win Condition Robustness are critical. Defensive roles (Intimidate, Redirection) are less critical unless they enable sweepers.

## Bulky Offense

**Core:** Heavy hitters with significant bulk that can trade into common threats and win extended exchanges.

**Strategy:** Field Pokemon that can take hits and return OHKOs or 2HKOs. Less reliant on speed control or setup — the win condition is winning trades through superior stats and typing.

**Key Moves:** High-power STABs, recovery moves, Protect, setup moves that can be clicked under pressure

**Strengths:** Punishes fragile teams, ignores speed control wars, can outlast Hyper Offense
**Weaknesses:** Can be outmaneuvered by fast teams if they fail to trade efficiently; vulnerable to strong setup sweepers if unable to KO before boosts accumulate

**Champions profile:** Needs at least 2 bulky attackers with strong defensive typing, a pivot or recovery option, and a way to punish setup (Haze, Taunt, or raw damage pressure).

## Tailwind Hyper Offense

**Core:** Fast Tailwind setter + multiple mid-tier attackers that become blazing-tier under Tailwind.

**Strategy:** Set Tailwind Turn 1. Overwhelm with immediate, hard-hitting attacks before the opponent can stabilize. Games are decided in 3-4 turns.

**Key Moves:** Tailwind, Fake Out, Protect, high-power STABs, spread moves

**Strengths:** Mid-tier attackers jump to effectively blazing, forces reactive plays, punishes slow teams
**Weaknesses:** Brittle if Tailwind setter is KO'd Turn 1; struggles against other Tailwind or naturally blazing teams; 4-turn Tailwind window creates urgency

**Champions profile:** Needs a reliable Tailwind setter (preferably fast or with Focus Sash), 2-3 mid-tier attackers with strong offensive stats, and at least one backup plan if Tailwind fails.

## Terrain-based

**Core:** Terrain setter + abusers that benefit from terrain effects (e.g., Psychic Terrain blocks priority; Grassy Terrain provides passive recovery and boosts Grass moves; Electric Terrain boosts Electric moves and blocks Sleep).

**Strategy:** Establish terrain and leverage its effects for the duration. Terrain setters can be ability-based (e.g., Electric Surge) or move-based.

**Key Moves:** Terrain-setting moves/abilities, terrain-boosted attacks, priority-blocking synergy, recovery moves in Grassy Terrain

**Strengths:** Consistent passive value, blocks common strategies (priority, Sleep), powers up specific types
**Weaknesses:** Terrain can be overwritten by opposing terrain; abusers are weaker without terrain active; terrain setters may be predictable

**Champions profile:** Needs a terrain setter (ability or move), at least one abuser that meaningfully benefits, and a plan for when terrain is overwritten. Consider this a variant of Weather or Goodstuffs depending on team construction.

## Goodstuffs / Balance

**Core:** No specific gimmick — strong, versatile Pokemon with good matchups across the meta.

**Strategy:** Flexible game plan. Can adapt to different opponents. Wins through better plays, not a specific combo.

**Key Traits:** Good type coverage, multiple win conditions, Intimidate support, speed control options, no single point of failure

**Strengths:** No auto-loss matchups, adaptable, doesn't rely on one combo
**Weaknesses:** No overwhelming advantage in any specific matchup, requires strong piloting

**Champions profile:** No single engine. Evaluate based on whether the bring-4 consistently threatens OHKOs/2HKOs on diverse defensive profiles. Well-roundedness is the goal.

## Scoring Anchors for Archetype Coherence

**Mega Evolution note:** When evaluating archetype fit for a team with a Mega Evolution, use the Mega form's typing and ability. A Mega may patch archetype weaknesses (e.g., Mega Charizard X's Fire/Dragon typing for a Sun team needing Ground immunity). Two-Mega teams must have distinct, viable modes for each Mega — if one Mega's mode is non-viable, score archetype coherence down by one tier.

Use these anchors when assigning a score to the **Archetype Coherence** dimension in `SKILL.md`:

- **5 (Optimal):** Clean archetype execution with all expected elements and no dilution.
- **4 (Strong):** Clear archetype with minor gaps that don't undermine the core plan.
- **3 (Acceptable):** Archetype is present but under-supported, or the team is Goodstuffs/Balance with decent flexibility.
- **2 (Weak):** Conflicting signals, missing critical archetype pieces, or an unsupported gimmick.
- **1 (Critical flaw):** No coherent archetype or strategy. The team lacks a unifying game plan.

**Distinguishing Goodstuffs from "no archetype":** A Goodstuffs/Balance team (score 3+) has no single gimmick but covers core VGC roles (Intimidate, speed control, Fake Out/redirection), has multiple independent win conditions, and can threaten diverse defensive profiles. Score 1–2 is reserved for teams that not only lack an engine but also cannot execute basic VGC functions or threaten common defensive profiles.

### Dual-Mode Scoring Notes

A dual-mode team (e.g., Tailwind + Trick Room) scores 4-5 **only if** modes are cleanly separated:
- Each mode has speed-appropriate attackers (mid-tier for Tailwind, TR-tier for Trick Room).
- No speed-tier conflicts within a single bring-4.
- Support Pokemon are speed-agnostic (appear in both modes).

**Important:** Support Pokemon appearing in multiple bring-4 modes is intentional flexibility, not "bleeding." Evaluators should not penalize speed-agnostic supports (e.g., Intimidate users, redirectors, Fake Out users) for appearing in both modes. "Bleeding" specifically refers to **attackers** with mismatched speed tiers fielded in the wrong mode (e.g., a mid-tier attacker in the TR bring-4, or a TR-tier Pokemon in the Tailwind bring-4). Score 2-3 only when attackers are in the wrong mode.

### Anti-Meta / Gimmick Scoring Notes

A team may deliberately lack a recognized archetype but have a clearly articulated anti-meta plan (e.g., 6 Pokemon all carrying specific coverage to counter the top 3 meta threats, or a team built around a known meta weakness such as common Ice-type leads). If the team:
- Has a written or clearly inferable anti-meta thesis,
- Documents specific matchup targeting against prevalent threats, and
- Would score 1-2 purely on archetype coherence,

then score **3** (functional anti-meta plan) or **4** (strongly targeted with evidence of viability) instead of defaulting to 1-2.

### Goodstuffs / Balance Scoring Notes

Goodstuffs teams are scored on well-roundedness, not a single engine:
- Score 5: All roles covered, multiple independent win conditions, no concentration risk, strong type chart.
- Score 3: Functional but missing one or two roles, or overly reliant on individual Pokemon strength rather than synergy.
- Score 1: No unifying strategy and unable to threaten common defensive profiles.
